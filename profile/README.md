# usefliq

We think getting paid should be as fast as sending a message.

</div>

<div align="center">

## The Short Version

usefliq is building the payment layer for internet-native work.

If a client can send “Looks great” in two seconds, that same client should be able to pay in two seconds.

No ceremony.
No dashboard maze.
No emotional-support follow-ups.

## The Real Problem

Most people do not get paid late because they did bad work.
Most people get paid late because the workflow is broken.

You finish the project in one place.
You explain scope in another place.
You create an invoice in a third place.
You paste account details into a fourth place.
You wait in a fifth place.

Every handoff creates friction.
Every friction point creates delay.
Every delay point creates that one message nobody wants to send.

“Hey, just checking in on payment.”

You know the one.

## What usefliq Changes

We merge communication and payment into one system.

Conversation is not separate from execution.
Execution is not separate from settlement.
Settlement is not separate from proof.

Same thread.
Same context.
Same outcome.

Work done.
Payment done.
Move on.

## Flagship Product

### fliq

fliq turns messages into enforceable, payable workflows.

You and a client agree in chat.
You issue payment intent in that same flow.
They approve and pay instantly.
Proof is written on-chain.

No switching tabs.
No copy-paste gymnastics.
No “sent from my invoicing app” era energy.

## What It Feels Like

Without fliq, work can feel like a relay race where every baton handoff is an app.
Chat app.
Notes app.
Invoice app.
Bank app.
Calendar reminder.
Existential sigh.

With fliq, you finish work and money arrives while you are still in creator mode.

No chasing.
No uncertainty.
No fake-politeness drafts in your notes app.

## Design Principles

### One Flow

If users must mentally stitch tools together, we failed.

### Trust by Default

Identity is wallet-based.
Messages are signed.
Records are verifiable.
Proof exists even when memory gets creative.

### Speed Is a Feature

Settlement should match the speed of modern communication.
If your message is instant and your money is not, the system is lying to you.

### Fewer Clicks, More Outcomes

We are not interested in adding admin theater.
We are interested in deleting unnecessary steps.

## Architecture Direction

Communication runs on peer-to-peer rails.
Payments settle on Solana.
Files and artifacts are content-addressed.
Compliance logic lives in the flow from day one.

Not bolted on.
Not patched in later.
Designed together from first principles.

## Contribution Guidelines

The community can get involved by improving product quality, developer experience, and clarity.

Open an issue when you spot friction in a workflow.
Open a pull request when you can propose a concrete fix.
Keep changes focused, readable, and testable.
Explain why a change matters, not only what changed.
Be respectful, direct, and collaborative in reviews.

If you are unsure where to start, begin with documentation improvements and small workflow fixes.
